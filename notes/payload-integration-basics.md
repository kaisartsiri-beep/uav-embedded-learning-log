# Payload Integration Basics

This note summarizes the basic factors involved in integrating a payload into a UAV platform.

## What Is a Payload?

A payload is the useful equipment carried by a UAV to complete its mission.

Examples:

- RGB camera
- Thermal camera
- LiDAR sensor
- GNSS module
- Multispectral camera
- Onboard computer
- Communication equipment
- Cargo

## Payload Integration Is Not Just Mounting

Payload integration is not only about attaching a device to the drone. The payload affects the full UAV system.

Important factors include:

- Weight
- Center of gravity
- Power consumption
- Communication interface
- Mounting method
- Vibration
- Cooling
- Data storage
- Reliability
- Environmental conditions

## Weight

A heavier payload reduces flight time and may affect stability.

The UAV must be able to safely carry the payload without exceeding its limits.

## Center of Gravity

Payload position affects the center of gravity.

If the payload is placed too far forward, backward, or to one side, it can affect stability and flight performance.

## Power Requirements

Each payload needs a specific voltage and current.

The power system must be able to supply the payload without overloading the battery or power distribution system.
